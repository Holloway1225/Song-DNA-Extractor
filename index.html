How it works
The extractor runs in two passes.

Pass 1 — Structure read: identifies the source song's actual situation — the setting, the speaker's physical posture, the central object or action, and the core contradiction. This is shown to you so you can see what the tool is reading from.

Pass 2 — New concept: builds a fresh, generator-ready concept that carries the same emotional logic but in a different setting, posture, and object than the source — and different from whatever you've flagged as overused above. It does not summarize or quote the original.

Output target: 85–135 words, one paragraph, no headings, no bullets.
"A person has already walked out but keeps standing at the curb with their keys in hand because nobody inside will admit what happened. The rooms behind them are still lit. The damage has been cleaned up just enough to look normal from the window. The new song should explore the strange punishment of leaving before being believed."
