How it works
The reader runs in two passes.

Pass 1 — Structure read: identifies the song's actual situation — the setting, the speaker's physical posture, the central object or action, and the core contradiction. Shown to you so you can see what the tool is reading from.

Pass 2 — What it's about: writes a clear paragraph describing what this song is about — its situation, its wound, and the tension underneath it — entirely in fresh words. It never quotes the lyrics, never reuses the song's phrasing or hooks, and does not invent a different scene. It tells you what the song you pasted is saying.

Output target: 85–135 words, one paragraph, no headings, no bullets.
"This song is about someone who keeps showing up to a celebration they no longer believe in, holding a drink they won't raise and a smile that's costing them. The thing they once organized their whole life around turned out to be hollow, and it failed so gradually they can't name the moment it broke. The tension is that they keep attending the rituals anyway — because leaving would mean admitting the loss is total — even though every polite exchange confirms exactly that. The song sits in the gap between the loyalty they perform and the conviction that quietly left them."
