How it works
The extractor reads the song, identifies the real situation underneath it — the wound, the contradiction, the thing the speaker can't stop reaching for — then turns that structure into one fresh, generator-ready concept.

It does not summarize. It does not copy hooks or phrasing. It finds a new scene that carries the same emotional logic so your next song starts from a real idea, not a description of one.

Output target: 85–135 words, one paragraph, no headings, no bullets.
"A person has already walked out but keeps standing at the curb with their keys in hand because nobody inside will admit what happened. The rooms behind them are still lit. The damage has been cleaned up just enough to look normal from the window. The new song should explore the strange punishment of leaving before being believed."
